# Code of Conduct

## 1. FDS Project Rules

Hotaru is a project under the Field of Dreams Studio (FDS) ecosystem.

Participation in official Hotaru spaces should follow the relevant FDS rules and policies published at:

https://doc.fds.moe/policies/

This includes expectations around respectful communication, maintaining a fair and harm-free collaboration environment, protecting private or internal information, and avoiding behavior that disrupts the project or community.

If this document and official FDS policies appear to conflict, the applicable FDS policy takes priority.

## 2. Open Participation

Hotaru welcomes contributions from everyone.

You do not need to be an FDS member to:

- Open issues
- Submit pull requests
- Improve documentation
- Report bugs
- Suggest features
- Ask questions
- Review code or design ideas

FDS members and non-members are both encouraged to participate constructively. All contributors are expected to communicate respectfully and work in good faith.

## 3. Expected Conduct

In Hotaru project spaces, contributors should:

- Be respectful and patient
- Focus criticism on code, design, documentation, and ideas rather than people
- Give and receive feedback constructively
- Respect maintainers' time and project priorities
- Avoid harassment, personal attacks, discrimination, trolling, spam, or other disruptive behavior
- Avoid leaking private, confidential, or internal information

Project maintainers may moderate issues, pull requests, discussions, or other project spaces when necessary to maintain a healthy collaboration environment.

## 4. AI-Assisted Contributions

Hotaru allows AI-assisted development, but contributors remain responsible for the work they submit.

The project follows the AI collaboration principles described in the root `readme.md`, including the expectation that authors understand and can explain their submitted code, documentation, tests, and design decisions.

Reviewers may ask contributors to explain or walk through AI-assisted work. Such requests are part of normal technical review and should be treated as requests for evidence of understanding, not as personal accusations.

Contributors should not submit AI-generated work that they cannot understand, maintain, or defend in review.

## 5. Reporting Problems

If you see behavior that violates this Code of Conduct or relevant FDS
policies, please contact the maintainers.

You may report problems through:

- GitHub issues or discussions, when appropriate
- Direct contact with project maintainers
- FDS contact channels listed in the project documentation

Maintainers will handle reports as respectfully and confidentially as reasonably possible.

## 6. Enforcement

Maintainers may take reasonable action to protect the project community,
including:

- Asking participants to clarify or adjust their behavior
- Editing, hiding, or removing inappropriate comments
- Closing or locking discussions
- Rejecting contributions
- Temporarily or permanently limiting participation in project spaces
- Referring serious issues to the appropriate FDS process

The goal of enforcement is to protect a fair, constructive, and harm-free
collaboration environment.
